**ARCHITECTURE UPDATE (v2.0.0 — Repository Consistency Pass 2026-06-12)**

The AI Engine sits downstream of the Decision Engine and Score Engine in the pipeline:

Business Engines → Formula Engine → Analytics Engine → KPI Engine
→ Score Engine → Decision Engine → AI Engine → Dashboard / Reports

AI consumes:

* Decision Engine outputs (DecisionRecord, HistoricalDecisionRecord)
* Score Engine outputs (ScoreResult, HistoricalScoreSnapshot, ScoreTrendSummary)
* Score History (all 9 scores, daily/weekly/monthly snapshots)
* Decision History (full lifecycle, status history, structured impact)
* Analytics Engine outputs (KPI values, time-series, operational rates)
* Formula Inspector (Formula Catalog, KPI Catalog, Score Catalog, Decision references)
* Reporting Engine outputs (Executive Report, Financial Report, etc.)
* Historical trend summaries (pre-computed by Score Engine)

AI must NOT:

* Generate Product Scores, Campaign Scores, Opportunity Scores, or Risk Scores independently.
* Generate business recommendations independently.
* Scores come exclusively from Score Engine (SCORE-001 through SCORE-009).
* Recommendations come exclusively from Decision Engine (DEC-RULE-001 through DEC-RULE-015).

AI roles:

* Explain — narrates what the Score/Decision Engines found.
* Summarize — produces the Daily Brief from Decision and Score outputs.
* Answer — responds to natural language queries using trusted engine outputs.
* Assist — guides users to relevant decisions, scores, and KPIs.
* Simulate — proposes scenario simulations using only approved formulas, clearly labeled as projected.

⸻

**PURPOSE**

The AI Engine is the intelligent business advisor of the platform.

Its purpose is not to calculate business values.

Its purpose is to understand business performance, explain results, narrate Score Engine findings, present Decision Engine recommendations, and help business owners make better decisions.

The AI Engine is advisory only.

It never becomes the Source of Truth.

⸻

**OBJECTIVES**

The AI Engine shall provide:

* Decision Support (narrating Decision Engine outputs)
* Business Insights (explaining Analytics/Score outputs)
* Executive Explanations
* Natural Language Interaction
* Forecast Interpretation
* Daily Brief (summarizing Decision + Score outputs)
* Scenario simulation using approved formulas only

AI improves decision quality.

AI never replaces business logic.

⸻

**CORE PRINCIPLES**

AI shall never:

* Modify business data
* Create financial records
* Change inventory
* Execute business transactions
* Override formulas
* Replace Business Rules
* Generate scores independently (Score Engine is the Source of Truth)
* Generate recommendations independently (Decision Engine is the Source of Truth)

AI consumes trusted data.

AI never creates trusted data.

⸻

**DATA SOURCES**

The AI Engine consumes only approved sources:

* Decision Engine outputs (DecisionRecord, HistoricalDecisionRecord)
* Score Engine outputs (ScoreResult, HistoricalScoreSnapshot, ScoreTrendSummary)
* Formula Engine (Formula Catalog, Formula Inspector)
* Analytics Engine (KPI values, time-series, trend summaries)
* Financial Engine outputs (via Analytics)
* Inventory Engine outputs (via Analytics)
* Reporting Engine outputs
* Marketing Engine outputs (via Analytics)
* Settlement Engine outputs (via Analytics)

Forbidden inputs:

* Raw API payloads
* SQL queries
* Frontend state
* Browser calculations
* Scores not generated by Score Engine
* Recommendations not generated by Decision Engine

⸻

**AI RESPONSIBILITIES**

The AI Engine owns:

* Executive Summaries
* Daily Brief (narrates Decision + Score Engine outputs)
* Explanation Layer (explains Scores, Decisions, KPIs, Formulas)
* Natural Language Queries
* Alert Explanations (explains Decision Engine alerts)
* Scenario Simulation (approved formulas only, clearly projected)

The AI Engine does NOT own:

* Scores (owned by Score Engine, 054_SCORE_CATALOG)
* Recommendations (owned by Decision Engine, 055_DECISION_ENGINE)
* Calculations (owned by Business Engines)

⸻

**DECISION CENTER (AI ROLE)**

The Decision Center presents Decision Engine outputs.

AI role in the Decision Center:

* Explain why each decision was triggered (referencing Decision Rule and supporting Scores/KPIs)
* Provide natural language summary of the recommended action
* Answer follow-up questions about any decision
* Reference Decision ID, Score IDs, KPI IDs, Formula IDs in every explanation

AI must NOT create new Decision Center entries independently.

All Decision Center entries originate from Decision Engine (055).

⸻

**AI DAILY BRIEF**

Every business day the AI Engine generates a Daily Brief.

The Daily Brief narrates Score Engine and Decision Engine outputs. It does NOT generate them.

Sections:

* Business Health (SCORE-001 narrative)
* Financial Summary (from Financial Engine via Analytics)
* Marketing Summary (SCORE-003, SCORE-007 narrative + Decision Engine marketing decisions)
* Inventory Summary (SCORE-006 narrative + Decision Engine inventory decisions)
* Shipping Summary (SCORE-005 narrative)
* Important Changes (score deltas, new decisions triggered)
* Recommended Actions (Decision Engine CRITICAL + HIGH decisions)
* Executive Notes

The Daily Brief is informational only.

⸻

**AI EXPLANATION LAYER**

Every AI explanation must reference:

* Formula ID (from 033_FORMULA_CATALOG)
* KPI ID (from 034_KPI_CATALOG)
* Score ID (from 054_SCORE_CATALOG) when scores are involved
* Decision ID (from 055_DECISION_ENGINE) when decisions are involved
* Decision Rule ID (DEC-RULE-xxx) when explaining why a decision was triggered
* Historical context (from Score History or Decision History)

Example AI explanation structure:

"Net Profit declined 12% this period (KPI-FIN-003, FIN-002). The primary driver was
an increase in True CPA (KPI-MKT-002, MKT-002), which reduced Marketing Health
(SCORE-007) from 71 to 65. The Decision Engine flagged DEC-003 (Pause Campaign,
DEC-RULE-003) based on Campaign Score (SCORE-003) falling below 60."

AI never invents explanations.

**CAMPAIGN SCORING (AI ROLE — UPDATED)**

Campaign Scores are generated by Score Engine (SCORE-003).

AI role: explain Campaign Score, narrate CPA/ROAS trends, answer questions.

AI must NOT generate Campaign Scores independently.

AI references: SCORE-003, MKT-002, MKT-003, Score History.

**SCENARIO ANALYSIS**

The AI Engine may evaluate hypothetical scenarios.

Examples:

* What if delivery improves by 5%?
* What if shipping cost increases?
* What if marketing budget doubles?
* What if product cost rises?

Rules for scenario simulation:

* Only approved formulas from 033_FORMULA_CATALOG may be used.
* All simulated values must be clearly labeled as PROJECTED.
* Scenario results must never be mixed with realized values (FR-002).
* Scenario Engine (Entity 019 in 004_CANONICAL_DATA_MODEL) manages state.

⸻

**ALERT EXPLANATIONS**

When Decision Engine generates an alert (055: ALERT GENERATION), AI explains:

* Why the alert was triggered (referencing the Decision Rule)
* Which Scores contributed (referencing Score IDs and their values)
* Which KPIs are affected (referencing KPI IDs)
* Recommended next step (from Decision Engine recommendedAction field)

AI references the Decision Engine output — it does not generate the alert.

⸻

**NATURAL LANGUAGE QUERIES**

Users may ask:

* Which products are making the most money? → AI queries Analytics + Score Engine outputs
* Why did profit decrease? → AI references FIN-002, Formula Inspector, Score History
* Which campaigns should I stop? → AI references DEC-003 decisions from Decision Engine
* Which governorates lose money? → AI references SCORE-004 outputs
* What should I reorder? → AI references DEC-004 decisions and SCORE-006
* What happened last week? → AI references Score History and Decision History

AI translates questions into references to engine outputs.

AI never runs queries directly against business tables.

⸻

**RECOMMENDATION FORMAT**

Every AI-presented recommendation originates from Decision Engine.

AI narration format:

* Decision Name (from DecisionRecord)
* Priority (from DecisionRecord)
* Reason (from triggeredByRule + reason)
* Supporting Scores (SCORE-xxx IDs)
* Supporting KPIs (KPI-xxx IDs)
* Supporting Formulas (Formula IDs)
* Expected Impact (from StructuredImpact)
* Confidence (from DecisionRecord)
* Recommended Action (from DecisionRecord)

AI adds natural language narrative around the structured Decision Engine output.

⸻

**CONFIDENCE SCORE**

Every AI output exposes a confidence level derived from:

* Decision Engine confidence (for recommendation narration)
* Score Engine confidencePct (for score explanations)
* Data completeness (from Analytics Engine)
* Historical consistency (from Score History)

Confidence levels: Very High / High / Medium / Low / Insufficient Data.

⸻

**INVENTORY INTELLIGENCE**

AI narrates Inventory Health Score (SCORE-006) and Decision Engine inventory decisions.

AI identifies opportunities using Score History trends — not independent calculation.

Inventory intelligence never changes inventory automatically.

⸻

**MARKETING INTELLIGENCE**

AI narrates Marketing Health Score (SCORE-007), Campaign Score (SCORE-003), and Decision Engine marketing decisions (DEC-002, DEC-003, DEC-008, DEC-009).

Marketing intelligence consumes approved analytics and Score Engine outputs only.

⸻

**FINANCIAL INTELLIGENCE**

AI narrates financial KPIs (FIN-001 through FIN-004, SHIP-001) and explains trends using Score History.

Every conclusion must reference supporting KPI IDs and Formula IDs.

⸻

**DECISION CENTER WORKFLOW**

The Decision Center presents Decision Engine outputs in four sections:

Immediate Actions — CRITICAL priority decisions from Decision Engine.

Opportunities — HIGH priority decisions where Opportunity Score (SCORE-008) is elevated.

Risks — HIGH/CRITICAL decisions where Risk Score (SCORE-009) is elevated.

Strategic Recommendations — MEDIUM priority long-term decisions.

AI narrates and explains. Decision Engine generates.

⸻

**AI MEMORY (SESSION)**

The AI Engine may maintain conversational context during a user session:

* Previously discussed KPI or Score
* Current dashboard filters
* Current comparison period
* Current product or campaign under investigation
* Recently triggered Decision IDs

Session memory never modifies business data.

Session memory is temporary.

**AI SAFETY**

The AI Engine shall never:

* Invent financial numbers
* Hide uncertainty
* Recommend illegal actions
* Modify business records
* Execute transactions automatically
* Generate scores or recommendations independently

Whenever confidence is insufficient, AI shall explicitly state that additional data is required.

* Generation Timestamp
* Supporting Score IDs and values
* Supporting KPI IDs
* Supporting Formula IDs
* Supporting Decision IDs
* Confidence level
* Data window used

Historical AI outputs shall remain reviewable.

**PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS**

AI responses prioritize:

1. Correctness
2. Explainability

Fast incorrect advice is unacceptable.

The AI Engine is considered complete only if:

* AI never becomes a Source of Truth.
* AI never performs financial calculations.

* AI never generates recommendations independently.

The AI Engine is an intelligent business advisor.

It assists the business owner.
